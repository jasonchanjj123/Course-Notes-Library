# Financial Market Course Materials

## Directory Overview
This directory serves as a repository for instructional and study materials for a course on **Financial Markets and Institutions**. The contents are primarily PDF documents consisting of lecture slides and presentation materials that cover the fundamental concepts of financial systems, valuation techniques, and market dynamics.

## Key Files
- **Root Directory Chapters:**
  - `2026 FM PPT_Chapter01.pdf` through `Chapter09.pdf`: A series of presentation slides for a "Financial Markets and Institutions" course (based on the 8th Edition by Saunders, Cornett, and Erhemjamts). Covers introductory market concepts, primary vs. secondary markets, money vs. capital markets, and derivative security markets.
- **Course Material Subdirectory:**
  - `FM The Time Value of Money(Part 1 & Part 2.Examples).pdf`: Comprehensive guide to time travel rules for cash flows, NPV calculations, annuities, and perpetuities.
  - `2022 Valuing Bonds.pdf` & `2022 Valuing Stocks.pdf`: Focused modules on the valuation of debt and equity instruments.
  - `Exchange Rates and the Foreign Exchange Market.pdf`: Analysis of international currency markets and exchange rate risk.
  - `Interest Rates and Money Markets.pdf`: Examination of how interest rates are determined and the role of money market instruments.
  - `FM Discounted Cash Flow Valuation(Part 3.Examples).pdf`: Practical examples and advanced applications of DCF valuation.

## Usage
The contents of this directory are intended for educational purposes:
- **Study Reference:** Use the chapter-wise slides to review core financial concepts and market structures.
- **Problem Solving:** Refer to the "Examples" and "Time Value of Money" documents for formulas and step-by-step solutions to financial valuation problems.
- **Research:** Utilize the detailed market instrument data (e.g., Money Market vs. Capital Market instruments) for historical or theoretical analysis.

The materials are structured to support both foundational learning (Chapter 1, TVM) and specialized topics (Bonds, Stocks, FX), making it a comprehensive resource for understanding the financial ecosystem.
